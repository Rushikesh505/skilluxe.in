COURSE DETAILS: MASTER OF BUSINESS ADMINISTRATION (MBA)

WHAT DOES AN MBA CAREER ENTAIL?

An MBA (Master of Business Administration) is a graduate degree focused on business management. It equips students with essential skills in leadership, decision-making, and strategic thinking. MBA graduates often pursue careers in management, consulting, finance, marketing, and entrepreneurship. The program typically includes coursework in finance, marketing, human resources, operations, and business strategy.

WHO CAN DO AN MBA?

Candidates with a bachelor's degree in any discipline can pursue an MBA. This includes graduates from various fields such as:

  • B.Tech/B.E.
  • B.Sc. (Bachelor of Science)
  • B.Com. (Bachelor of Commerce)
  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)
  • BA (Bachelor of Arts)
  • Any other degree holders

REQUIRED STUDY FOR MBA

Candidates generally need to complete a bachelor's degree and may be required to take entrance exams such as:

  • CAT (Common Admission Test)
  • XAT (Xavier Aptitude Test)
  • MAT (Management Aptitude Test)
  • CMAT (Common Management Admission Test)

Many universities also conduct group discussions and personal interviews as part of the admission process.

COURSE DETAILS: CHARTERED ACCOUNTANT (CA)

WHAT DOES A CA CAREER ENTAIL?

A Chartered Accountant (CA) is a professional designation in the finance and accounting sectors. CAs are trained in financial management, accounting, auditing, taxation, and financial reporting. They often work in various sectors, including corporate finance, investment banking, auditing firms, and taxation services. A career as a CA involves providing financial advice, conducting audits, and ensuring compliance with laws and regulations.

WHO CAN BECOME A CA?

Candidates who have completed their higher secondary education (10+2) can pursue the CA course. It is also open to graduates in any discipline, including:

  • B.Com. (Bachelor of Commerce)
  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)
  • B.Sc. (Bachelor of Science)
  • B.Tech/B.E. (Bachelor of Technology/Engineering)
  • Any other degree holders

REQUIRED STUDY FOR CA

To become a CA, candidates must complete a series of examinations and practical training. The process typically involves:

  • Clearing the CA Foundation Course (after 10+2)
  • Completing the CA Intermediate Course
  • Undergoing Articleship training (practical training under a registered CA)
  • Clearing the CA Final Course

Candidates must also register with the Institute of Chartered Accountants of India (ICAI) to pursue the CA course.

COURSE DETAILS: CHARTERED FINANCIAL ANALYST (CFA)

WHAT DOES A CFA CAREER ENTAIL?

The Chartered Financial Analyst (CFA) designation is a globally recognized credential for investment professionals. CFAs are experts in investment analysis, portfolio management, and financial research. They often work in asset management, investment banking, and financial consulting, providing insights and recommendations on investments and financial markets.

WHO CAN BECOME A CFA?

Candidates interested in pursuing the CFA designation should have a bachelor's degree or equivalent work experience. This includes graduates from various fields such as:

  • B.Com. (Bachelor of Commerce)
  • BBA (Bachelor of Business Administration)
  • B.Sc. (Bachelor of Science)
  • B.Tech/B.E. (Bachelor of Technology/Engineering)
  • Any other degree holders with relevant experience

REQUIRED STUDY FOR CFA

The CFA program consists of three levels of exams. Candidates must pass all three levels to earn the CFA charter. Requirements include:

  • Level I: Introduction to investment tools and concepts.
  • Level II: Application of investment analysis.
  • Level III: Portfolio management and wealth planning.

Candidates are also required to have four years of qualified work experience in investment decision-making.
